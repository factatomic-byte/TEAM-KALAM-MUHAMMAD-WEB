The Project

What is ATM?

A smarter way to manage city traffic — combining AI, computer vision, and real-time data to replace outdated fixed-timer signals.

Our project, Automated Traffic Management (ATM), is an AI-powered system designed to optimize traffic flow and improve road safety in smart cities.

Traditional traffic lights operate on fixed, pre-set timings — completely blind to actual traffic conditions. Our system sees the road in real time and adapts. Lanes with more vehicles get more green-light time. Lanes with fewer vehicles wait less.

Built on a Raspberry Pi with camera modules, the system is affordable, scalable, and ready for real-world deployment across smart city intersections.

📷

Real-Time Vision

Camera modules capture live traffic footage processed instantly by AI for vehicle counting and density analysis.

🚦

Adaptive Signal Timing

Green-light duration is dynamically allocated based on actual lane density — no more one-size-fits-all timers.

🚨

Violation Detection

Detects wrong-lane travel, wrong-direction movement, and red-light crossings with photographic evidence capture.

🤖

AI-Driven Intelligence

Computer vision models analyze traffic patterns, making instant decisions that optimize flow across all lanes.

System Architecture

How It Works

Five integrated stages take raw video feed and turn it into intelligent, real-time traffic management decisions.

📡

Capture

Camera modules mounted at intersections feed continuous live video to the Raspberry Pi processing unit.

🧠

Analyze

AI models count vehicles in each lane, measure density, and identify movement patterns in real time.

⚡

Decide

The system calculates optimal signal timings based on live density data — high traffic gets more green time.

🚦

Control

Traffic signals are adjusted automatically, cycle after cycle, without any manual operator intervention.

📸

Enforce

Rule violations are detected and photographed instantly — wrong lane, wrong direction, or red-light crossing.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Everything you need to know about how the ATM system works and what makes it different.

The system runs on a Raspberry Pi paired with camera modules at each intersection approach. It's specifically chosen for cost-effectiveness and low power consumption — making it feasible to deploy across hundreds of city intersections without prohibitive infrastructure costs.
In testing, our system achieved approximately 87% efficiency improvement over fixed-timer baselines in terms of vehicle throughput and average wait time reduction. Gains are highest during uneven traffic loads — for example, morning rush where one direction has 5× more vehicles than the other.
The current system detects three violation types: vehicles crossing during a red signal, vehicles traveling in the wrong lane, and vehicles moving in the wrong direction (e.g., going against one-way traffic). Each violation triggers an automatic photographic capture for evidence.
The AI model is trained on diverse lighting and weather conditions. Night operation is supported through infrared-capable cameras. Heavy rain or fog can reduce detection accuracy — adding additional sensor modalities like radar is part of the Phase 3 roadmap to address extreme conditions.
